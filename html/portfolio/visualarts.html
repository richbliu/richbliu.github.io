After the Masters

Charcoal, conte, and graphite on Paper, 18' x 36' Sometimes life isn't as beautiful, "shiny," or even as perfect as depicted by some of the more traditional old masters. A deeper insight reveals an area of transparency, where there is more to life than merely the classical outer facade. The three figures reference the three graces in both classical style and meaning, but show the same facade: all the pains, worries, and depression, are masked by seemingly beautiful bodies. As with art history, there has been tremendous change throughout the centuries, as artists began moving away from pure representation and classicism and approaching expressionism, impressionism, cubism, and futurism. Art began to take more meaning and convey deeper emotion, as the "curtain" of classicism was pulled aside. Thus, while classical representation often depicts striking realism, it doesn't necessarily define art or represent what art should be. Art should be what the artist sees and how the artist sees.

Apocalypse

Graphite and charcoal on heavy-press paper, 16' x 24' The hand morph represents humankind's deep impact on the environment and nature's subsequent backlash (However, feel free to interpret it according to your preference). Nature has not only retaliated through inclement weather and a dark premonition of the future (as reflected onto the tall skyscrapers), but has also CONSUMED the human being - leaving the hand as the only body part still intact, but soon to be quickly devoured. With the veins becoming roots and the fingers branching out into the dark sky, our fate is seemingly determined, as foreshadowed by the obelisk behind the hand (possibly representing god - up for many different interpretations). The future is dark and apocalyptic - IF WE DO NOT do anything about our environmental impact. The Lakes and Oceans - as reflected onto the skyscrapers- will soon become hurricanes, tornadoes, and tsunamis if we do not substantially reduce our carbon impact. Nature will overtake us and retaliate if we do not act now. Apocalypse.
